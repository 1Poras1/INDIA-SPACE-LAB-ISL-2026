### India Space Lab — Summer Internship & Technical Training (2026)

Completed the **45-day online Summer Internship & Technical Training Program 2026** conducted by India Space Lab (ISL), covering foundational and applied concepts across drone technology, satellites, rocketry, and remote sensing.

###  Modules Covered
- Advanced Drone Technology
- CanSat & CubeSat (Student Satellite Systems)
- Rocketry Fundamentals
- Remote Sensing & GIS
- Disaster Management

###  What I Learned
- **PID Control** — tuned proportional, integral, and derivative gains for a simulated drone's altitude control loop; learned to read overshoot, steady-state error, and damping behavior directly off a response curve, and how each gain term corrects a different failure mode.
- **Guidance & Path Tracking** — implemented a feedback guidance law to steer an autonomous system (boat simulation) along a desired path, and saw firsthand how removing the damping term causes oscillatory, unstable tracking.
- **Trajectory Tracking Under Disturbance** — as a self-directed bonus project, built a closed-loop simulation of a drone flying a figure-eight path using parametric equations, a combined feedforward + PID controller, and a time-varying wind disturbance — tuning gains until tracking error dropped to sub-meter RMSE.
- **Simulation & Visualization in Python** — practical experience with NumPy for the physics/control math and Matplotlib (including `FuncAnimation` and `ipywidgets`) for interactive, animated visualizations of controller behavior.

###  Scope & Context
This was an independent, industry-style online training initiative — not a government or ISRO-run research internship — focused on giving students hands-on exposure to space and allied technologies through project-based learning rather than formal research work. It served as a practical, applied complement to more theory-heavy coursework, especially around control systems and simulation.

###  Relevance to My Work
The control-systems and trajectory-tracking concepts from this program connect directly to my ongoing interest in drone applications, building on earlier work on a swarm-drone system for precision agriculture and crop monitoring. It's sharpened my focus specifically toward **guidance, navigation, and control (GNC)** as a direction to pursue further.
